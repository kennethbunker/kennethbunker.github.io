Research Interests

My research is situated in the fields of political behavior, comparative politics and political sociology. My PhD project investigates the implications of shifting social status hierarchies for political attitudes and behavior, with a focus on shifts in cultural structural hierarchies like gender and race/ethnicity. The focus is on the connection between perceptions of status threat and radical right voting on the one hand and on perceptions of status gains and voting for socioculturally progressive parties on the other hand. Further, I am interested in the program and strategy of populist radical right parties and their effects on mainstream parties.

I use quantitative methods such as survey experiments and text analysis. I am conducting my PhD research within an project funded by the Swiss National Science Foundation, led by Tarik Abou-Chadi.
